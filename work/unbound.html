UNBOUND

Designing a contemporary redesign of the QAGOMA website for art discovery and cultural engagement.

UNBOUND is an academic web design project where I redesigned the QAGOMA website as a more expressive and accessible digital experience for discovering exhibitions, events, gallery content, and cultural stories.

Project Overview

UNBOUND is a conceptual redesign of the QAGOMA website, developed as part of DECO7140 Introduction to Web Design. The project explored how a major cultural institution’s digital presence could be reimagined to feel more expressive, accessible, and engaging for contemporary audiences.

The redesign focused on improving the way users discover exhibitions, browse gallery content, explore events, read cultural stories, and move toward ticket booking. Rather than treating the website as a static information source, UNBOUND positions the gallery website as a more immersive digital experience that reflects the energy, experimentation, and visual richness of contemporary art.

The final website combines a bold editorial visual identity with clear navigation, responsive layouts, and front-end implementation. Photoshop and Illustrator supported the development of visual assets, image treatment, branding elements, and graphic direction, while Figma supported interface planning and layout design. The final prototype was developed using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript.

Challenge

UNBOUND began as a response to a common issue in cultural and gallery websites: they often hold rich visual content, but the user journey can feel fragmented. Visitors may need to move between exhibition pages, event listings, artist information, blogs, and booking options without a clear sense of flow.

The challenge was to design a digital platform that made contemporary art feel more discoverable, expressive, and accessible, while still supporting practical user goals such as browsing exhibitions, finding events, reading artist stories, and booking tickets.

Research & Insight

The research phase focused on understanding how gallery, museum, and cultural event websites organise visual content and guide users toward action. I analysed how these platforms present exhibitions, events, artist profiles, newsletters, and editorial content, paying close attention to where users might lose context or struggle to move between sections.

A key insight was that users need both inspiration and direction. The website needed to feel visually bold enough to reflect the energy of contemporary art, but also structured enough for users to quickly understand where to go next. This shaped the information architecture around five core areas: Home, Gallery, Events, Blog, and About Us.

Early Prototyping

Early prototyping focused on mapping the main user journeys: discovering a featured exhibition, browsing gallery content, exploring upcoming events, reading cultural stories, and moving toward ticket booking.

I explored layouts that could support both visual immersion and functional browsing. The homepage was designed as an entry point into the wider platform, using a large hero section, featured show promotion, horizontal image browsing, newsletter signup, and trending content modules to encourage continued exploration.

UI Design & Visual Language

The visual direction for UNBOUND was built around a bold black-and-white interface that allowed the artwork and performance imagery to become the main source of colour and emotion. Large typography, strong contrast, editorial spacing, and oversized imagery were used to create a contemporary gallery-like experience.

The brand identity uses a sculptural logo, dramatic serif typography, and a minimal monochrome navigation system to create a feeling that is both refined and expressive. The homepage hero section was designed to feel like a digital exhibition poster, with the title “UNBOUND” acting as a strong visual anchor above the featured show.

The design language aimed to communicate contemporary art and performance culture, editorial confidence, visual drama, clear hierarchy, and minimal but expressive interaction.

Navigation Design Principles

The navigation was designed around clarity and predictable movement. The main navigation includes Home, Gallery, Events, Blog, and About Us, giving users direct access to the platform’s key content areas. A search bar was included to support faster discovery, especially for users looking for specific artists, exhibitions, or events.

The design uses repeated interaction patterns such as underlined links, large call-to-action buttons, carousel browsing, and hover states to help users understand what is clickable. The “Book Tickets” button was given visual weight to make the main conversion pathway easy to identify without disrupting the artistic tone of the interface.

Frontend Development & Accessibility

The final prototype was developed using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with separate stylesheets for different page types including the homepage, gallery, events, blog, and about page. This helped keep the structure modular and easier to maintain.

Accessibility was considered through semantic HTML, descriptive image alt text, ARIA labels for navigation and search, and responsive media queries for smaller screens. The homepage includes accessible labels for the main navigation, search input, gallery navigation, and interactive carousel controls.

The responsive CSS adapts key sections such as navigation, image sliders, newsletter layouts, event cards, gallery grids, and footer content for mobile screens, supporting a more flexible browsing experience across devices.
