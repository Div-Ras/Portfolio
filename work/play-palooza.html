Play Palooza

A sports community app designed to help people find activities, connect with players, create games, and stay active after moving to a new place.

Overview

This freelance project explores how a mobile app can lower the barrier to joining sports and staying active, especially for people who have recently moved, are returning to fitness, or simply don't know where to find players at their level. Play Palooza is designed to help users choose a sport, find nearby activities, create games, connect with others, and keep learning — all within a single, community-driven platform.

Design Challenge

⚡ The central question is how to design an app that feels simple on the surface while supporting multiple complex tasks — joining a game, learning a sport, chatting, creating an event, and finding sessions nearby — without overwhelming the user.

Tools & Skills

Used Figma for interface design and prototyping, alongside competitor analysis, information architecture mapping, persona development, and mobile UI design. This work drew on skills in UX research, product structure thinking, visual direction setting, and high-fidelity flow development — translating early-stage research into a clear product direction for a sports community concept.

Research Scope

Reviewed existing sports and fitness apps to understand what users valued, where experiences fell short, and how platforms guided users into action. This shaped the feature set around five core areas: onboarding, event discovery, community connection, skill learning, and sports partner matching. Research focused on identifying the emotional barrier — that wanting to play a sport is often easier than actually joining one.

Methodology

The project moved through competitor analysis, persona development, information architecture, visual direction, and high-fidelity prototype development. The app structure was organised around key user actions: onboarding, choosing a sport, setting a goal, playing, learning, getting support, and managing a profile. The visual direction uses energetic, sport-led aesthetics with strong contrast and bright accent colours — motivating during onboarding, functional and focused across core screens. The high-fidelity prototype in Figma brings the main flow together from sign-in through to game creation and chat.

Industry Relevance

As fitness and community apps multiply, design teams face a recurring problem: How do you build a product that doesn't just track activity, but genuinely helps people feel less alone when trying to build an active routine? Early patterns from this project point to strategies such as designing onboarding that builds confidence rather than just collecting preferences, structuring discovery around social proximity rather than algorithmic feeds, and treating community connection as a first-class feature rather than an afterthought.
