About Me

Hi, I’m Divya.

I’m a UX/UI and interaction designer with a background in architecture, design research, and visual storytelling. My path into digital product design has always been shaped by one constant: creativity.

Before designing interfaces, I was trained to design spaces, studying architecture and learning how people move through, feel, and connect with their environments. That spatial way of thinking still shapes how I approach UX today, from accessibility and user flows to emotional experience and detail-driven design.

I grew up in Bhopal, India, a city known as the City of Lakes. Surrounded by water, old neighbourhoods, vernacular buildings, Mughal architecture, and a strong local culture, I became curious about how places influence people. My childhood home was a colonial-style bungalow shared with a large joint family, cousins, conversations, food, and constant activity. I had a small room where I painted, sketched, made things, and slowly understood that creativity was not just something I enjoyed. It was how I made sense of the world.

Over the years, my work has moved across architecture, tattooing, teaching, research, and digital design. Each of these experiences taught me something different about people. Architecture taught me structure and space. Tattooing taught me trust, precision, and how to translate someone’s story into something deeply personal. Teaching and mentoring taught me patience, empathy, and how to guide ideas from messy beginnings into meaningful outcomes. Today, I bring all of that into my design practice.

As a designer, I care about making digital experiences that feel clear, inclusive, and human. I enjoy working on problems where research, accessibility, visual design, and storytelling come together. I’m especially drawn to projects that improve how people access information, feel supported by technology, or connect with the spaces and communities around them.

Outside of work, I’m still very much a creative soul. I tattoo as a hobby, cook big dinners for friends and friends-of-friends, volunteer when I can, and recharge through swimming, hiking, long walks, sketching by the water, or simply lying on the grass. Moving to Brisbane has felt strangely familiar in the best way, with its river, outdoor life, and relaxed rhythm reminding me of the lake city I grew up in.

At the heart of everything I do, I’m driven by curiosity, care, and the belief that good design should not only solve problems, but make people feel seen.
